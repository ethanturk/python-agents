Farmers must remain vigilant against a host of pests and diseases that specifically target the Apiaceae family. Integrated Pest Management (IPM) strategies are often employed to minimize chemical usage while maximizing yield. Water management is another critical factor; the carrot is sensitive to both drought and waterlogging. The growing cycle varies by variety, but most commercial carrot crops are harvested within a specific window to ensure peak flavor and texture.
